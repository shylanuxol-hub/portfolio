about

"Collective Static" is a Fluxus-inspired participatory performance where individual marks become collective motion. Each participant is given an iPad and a stylus with only 30 seconds to draw. After each contribution, the brush, color, or thickness shifts slightly. The drawings become frames in an animation that no one sees until the very end.

The final animation is displayed on a CRT monitor ~ old technology paired with new, connected through a tangle of adapters and cables. The translation between media is part of the work: the way it travels, distorts, and re-emerges in another form.

concept

The piece reflects core fluxus values: process over product, collective over individual. Each person's contribution is absorbed into a larger flow. No one owns the piece. Every mark dissolves into accumulative, ephemeral authorship.

The setup itself is intentional ~ iPad, stylus, cables, adapters, CRT. The audience doesn't just see the drawings; they see the mediation, the delay, the translation. The machine is part of the work. This is also a commentary on the lengths people will go to for vintage aesthetics, and how past technologies keep resurfacing in the present.

process

The piece moves from action to watching, from playful immediacy to contemplative stillness. Participants engage without overthinking, acting quickly and lightly. The randomness and humor echo fluxus' anti-elitist spirit, yet my hope is that the structure still points to deeper questions about authorship, technology, and how we make art together.
