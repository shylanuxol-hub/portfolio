About

Doomscroller is a culture jamming browser game that critiques the attention economy. You play as an artist trying to create while being constantly interrupted by phone notifications, popups, and the endless pull of the scroll.

The game puts you in a familiar struggle ‚Äî the tension between wanting to make something meaningful and the constant digital noise competing for your attention. Every notification is a choice: engage with the distraction or try to stay focused on your work.

Concept

The piece emerged from thinking about how our devices have become sites of constant interruption. Social media platforms are designed to capture and hold attention, turning our focus into a commodity. As artists and creators, we're caught in a bind ‚Äî we need these platforms to share our work, but they're also actively working against the deep focus that creative work requires.

Doomscroller makes this tension playable. It's a small act of culture jamming ‚Äî using the language of games and notifications to critique the systems that deploy them.

Process

Built entirely in p5.js, the game uses simple mechanics to create an escalating sense of overwhelm. The notification system was designed to mimic real phone interruptions ‚Äî familiar sounds, urgent visual language, the dopamine hit of new information.

  • Developed in p5.js web editor
  • Custom notification and popup systems
  • Sound design using familiar UI audio cues
  • Responsive design for browser play
