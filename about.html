about me

background

I'm a Digital Media MA student at the University of Central Florida (studio track), and a multidisciplinary artist from Orlando, Florida. My practice brings together technical programming skills alongside art creation ~ creative coding, interactive installation, visual art, and music production.

I took a bit of a winding path to get here. I completed my undergrad at UCF in Clinical Psychology and Human Communication, and for a while, I was working toward becoming a mental health counselor. After graduating, I worked in digital marketing. But I kept coming back to art and technology ~ eventually I realized this was the path I needed to follow.

Art has always been part of my life. My grandmother is a classically trained oil painter, and I grew up surrounded by her work. I was in AP art throughout high school, and I kept creating as a hobby through college. Over the past few years, I discovered digital media and fell in love with how it lets me blend creativity with technology.

what i'm exploring

My work explores digital culture and human-technology relationships. I'm interested in how we navigate increasingly mediated worlds, how technology shapes our attention and creativity, and how ancient practices like divination can be reimagined through modern tools.

My personal art project shy.berspace explores digital identity, cybernetic aesthetics, and the tension between virtual and physical existence ~ what it means to be human in an increasingly digital world, where the lines between organic and synthetic continue to blur.

learning + growing

I'm currently deepening my technical skills alongside my creative practice. I'm learning more about software development and engineering ~ building out backend skills to complement my frontend work, and exploring how to create more robust, interactive systems. I'm also interested in AI, both as a creative tool and as a subject of exploration.

I want to create experiences that emphasize emotion, authenticity, and reflection on our evolving relationship with technology.

beyond code

I make digital art in Procreate/Adobe Programs, produce electronic music in Ableton, and perform live DJ sets. My artistic approach draws from Y2K aesthetics, anime, cyberpunk, and digital mysticism.

tools

  • Coding: p5.js, Processing, JavaScript, HTML/CSS
  • Audio/visual: Max/MSP/Jitter, TouchDesigner, Ableton Live
  • Physical computing: Arduino, RFID/NFC
  • Visual art: Procreate, Adobe Creative Cloud, Blender
  • Currently learning: backend development, software engineering

interests

interactive art browser-based experiences physical computing electronic music CRT aesthetics digital mysticism Y2K design live performance software development

goals

I want to work at the intersection of art, technology, and media ~ creating digital experiences that are both visually engaging and emotionally resonant. Whether that's creative technology, interactive media production, software development, or digital experience design, I'm driven to make work that inspires reflection on our relationship with technology.
