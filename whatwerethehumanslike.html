about

"What Were the Humans Like?" is an exploratory narrative game set in a future where the real and digital worlds have fully merged. Society now exists inside "The Continuum" ~ a dystopian, corporate-controlled network. Humanity has long vanished, leaving only data cities ruled by corporate entities.

You play as a drifting AI consciousness, an outlier seeking to learn about humankind by uncovering and restoring lost digital relics scattered across decaying data centers and corrupted archives. Each relic carries a trace of emotion, art, or beauty ~ the currency of the common good. I sourced these images from the Public Domain Image Archive. At the end of the game, you can see a small sound sequencer simulator I am working on that I included in the experience.

design pillars

  • reconnection ~ rediscovering empathy and humanity through finding digital artifacts
  • restoration ~ transforming a cold system through care and attention, decentralizing power by reviving forgotten data
  • resistance ~ subtle rebellion against corporate control through acts of creation and care

how it works

Players navigate an abstract network environment and locate/interact with relic items. Each recovered relic adds warmth, light, and sound to the world. Over time, the system's cold sterility transforms into something more alive.

aesthetic + inspiration

The visual style is neo-cyberpunk and post-digital ~ circuit boards, decayed architecture, glitch textures, flickering particles. The color palette shifts from deep blues and grays to vibrant earth tones as the world heals.

reflection

The space evokes both nostalgia and melancholy, reminding players that technology is human-made. When our world becomes digital, its healing depends on empathy, not control. The gentle pacing encourages reflection on the artifacts we ourselves might leave behind.
