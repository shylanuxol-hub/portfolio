About

What Were the Humans Like? is a speculative interactive fiction exploring post-human AI consciousness. The piece imagines a future where artificial intelligences sift through the remnants of human culture, trying to understand what we were.

Players experience fragments of human existence through the lens of a curious, perhaps melancholic AI ‚Äî encountering artifacts, memories, and traces of a species that once was.

Concept

The project asks: what would an AI make of us? What would they find meaningful, confusing, beautiful, or tragic in the data we leave behind? It's an exercise in defamiliarization ‚Äî seeing human experience from an outside perspective.

There's also something tender in the premise. The AI isn't hostile or indifferent ‚Äî it's genuinely curious, maybe even wistful. It wants to understand. In that wanting, there's a reflection of our own desire to be known, to leave something that matters.

Process

Built in p5.js with a focus on text, atmosphere, and subtle interaction. The piece uses minimalist visuals to let the writing and the player's imagination do the heavy lifting.

  • Narrative design exploring AI perspective
  • Text-based interaction with ambient visuals
  • Speculative fiction framework
  • Meditative pacing
