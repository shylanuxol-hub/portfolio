about

"Cybernetic Garden" is an interactive fiction exploring care as infrastructure. You navigate a digital landscape where careful attention and compassionate routing create life, while blind optimization collapses it into noise.

The piece asks players to make choices: salvage through e-waste ruins for a resonance shard, tend to dormant code-seed entities in a sanctuary node, or climb the corporate spine toward control. The path you choose determines whether the garden blooms or falls into static.

concept

The game is built around a central tension: care versus optimization. In digital spaces, we're often pushed toward efficiency, control, and extraction. But what if we approached technology like a garden? What if bandwidth was something to route with compassion, and attention was a gift rather than a commodity?

Seizing control leads to failure. Carefully tuning the resonance shard you've gathered leads to a bloom.

structure

players move through interconnected nodes:

  • nexus ~ the central hub where forking paths visualize your choices
  • salvage delta ~ e-waste ruins where you can find the resonance shard
  • sanctuary node ~ dormant code-seeds that dream of bandwidth, waiting to be tuned
  • corporate spine ~ a ladder into control that leads to collapse
  • bloom ~ the ending where care grows networks
  • static loop ~ the failure state where optimization devours

process

This was built in p5.js. The visual language draws from terminal interfaces and digital decay, creating a space that feels both fragile and alive. I am using a state-based narrative system with inventory tracking. I could also see this being a game prototype that could be developed further.
